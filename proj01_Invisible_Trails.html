Invisible Trails

Game apps hide a complex infrastructure characterized by opaque processes that are led by business models based on data-driven technologies whose aim is to collect a huge amount of data about the users. The thesis, through the case study of the freemium casual game apps available on Android smartphones, maps this infrastructure with the aim of making it visible to the users, highlighting the stakeholders and their relationship. The project is conceived as a digital alternative narrative to the experience of downloading an app. The artifact uses two metaphors: the patchwork and the journey; the first one shows how the trackers became part of the same whole once integrated into the games. Instead, the journey one stresses the paradox that while we are still to play, our data potentially travels all over the world and contributes to give materiality to this topic. Website
