Installation, Data visualization

S[a]nd me water

Water is a primary source that we can easily find, but in Somalia, millions of people are forced to move to search for it and save their lives. The installation shows how much the drought affects Somalia, one of the countries with the highest number of internal displacements, by linking the departure region with the number of people moved. According to the UNHCR, only this phenomenon caused 392.639 internal displacements from 2018 to October 2019. The tanks, used by the migrants during their migration journey, symbolize the regions; while the sand is used as a metaphor of the dryness. Higher is the level of the sand in the tank, more people moved and more the phenomenon was intense. The sand, glued inside and outside the containers, underlines this latest idea. The labels display the data to facilitate the users to understand the installation. Website
